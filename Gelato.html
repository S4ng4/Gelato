ORIGINS

When Ice
Became Art

Gelato was born in Renaissance Florence, around 1565, when architect Bernardo Buontalenti was commissioned by the Medici to create an extraordinary dessert for court celebrations. By combining cream, honey, and snow from the Apennines, the first modern version of gelato was born.

In the centuries that followed, Sicilian gelato makers carried the tradition across Europe — to Paris, Vienna, and Madrid. In 18th‑century Naples, gelato left royal courts and became food for the people, served in wafers on the streets.

Today Maestro Stefano Biasini — Italian Gelato Champion and international World Press Champion — brings this centuries‑old tradition to the heart of Philadelphia, reimagining every flavor with an engineer’s precision and an artist’s sensitivity.

LA TECNICA

L'Arte della Mantecatura

Churning is the soul of gelato: the process that transforms simple ingredients into a one-of-a-kind sensory experience. Every detail is controlled, every variable is calculated.

🥛
01
Selezione

Only fresh local whole milk, selected sugars, and exceptional Italian ingredients (including DOP when applicable). No additives, no preservatives. Quality starts with the raw materials.

🔥
02
Pastorizzazione

The base mix is pasteurized at a controlled temperature, then aged for 6–12 hours. This develops the creamy structure that defines artisanal gelato.

❄️
03
Mantecatura

Nel mantecatore, il mix si raffredda rapidamente mentre viene incorporata solo il 20–30% di ariaL'overrun: la percentuale di aria incorporata.
Meno aria = più densità = più sapore. — la metà rispetto all'ice cream industriale. Risultato: densità e intensità uniche.

🍨
04
Servizio

Conservato a −11°C (più caldo dell'ice cream), il gelato viene servito alla temperatura ottimale: abbastanza morbido da sprigionare tutti gli aromi, abbastanza compatto da reggere la cialda.
