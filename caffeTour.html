ORIGINS · 1580

When Venice
met Coffee

Coffee arrived in Europe in 1580, when Venetian merchants — along with botanist Prospero Alpino — first brought beans from Sidamo, Ethiopia. Venice was the first Italian city to embrace this dark, aromatic drink, nicknamed the devil’s beverage by its critics.

In the 17th century, the first coffee houses opened in Venice, Rome, and Florence — intellectual meeting places that would become the blueprint for modern European café culture. Venice’s Caffè Florian, founded in 1720, is still open today.

In Naples, coffee becomes a religion: dense, short, served piping hot in porcelain. In the North — in Turin — it becomes elegance, paired with chocolate in the legendary bicerin. Every Italian city has written its own grammar of coffee.

Gran Caffè L’Aquila brings this tradition to the heart of Philadelphia: five blends that tell the story of five cities, roasted in-house every day from beans selected in Italy — ready to become your perfect cup.

LA TECNICA

Roasted Fresh Every Day.
In-House.

Beans are selected and sorted in Italy, then roasted fresh every day in Gran Caffè L'Aquila’s roastery lab in Philadelphia. No compromises on freshness.

🌱
01
Selection

Every bean is selected and sorted in Italy — high-altitude Arabica and Super Premium Robusta from certified farms. The blend begins at the source.

🔥
02
Roasting

Each blend requires a precise, unique roast curve. From Torino’s delicate medium roast to Napoli’s intense dark roast — every profile is calibrated degree by degree.

⚗️
03
Blending

Arabica and Robusta are combined in precise ratios for each blend. The ratio determines body, crema, caffeine, and finish — every cup is a formula.

☕
04
Extraction

Ground fresh and extracted at 9 bars in 25–30 seconds: authentic Italian espresso. The hazelnut crema is the quality seal of every perfect shot.
